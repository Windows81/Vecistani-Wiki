Webhooky

Webhooky (stylised Webhòóky) was a webhook service for Rōblox developers to programmatically forward data to Discord, running from approx. 2018-02-24 to 2019-09-22p5. Webhooky was exploited by Vecistan as long-term message-interception project for its entire duration of service. It was an operation in the Rōblox metaverse that intercepted chatlogs from hundreds of games on Rōblox, including Arsenalp3 and Boho Salon.

Functionality

VisualPlugin provisioned a forwarding server on 000webhost which developers can direct webhook requests to Discord. To provision Webhooky, the developer would manually replace all links to http://discordapp.com/api/webhooks with http://le-blog.webutu.com/discordWebhook.php, keeping all other request parameters the same.

As Vecistan is coodinated as a centrally-planned economy with a strictly authoritarian government, Webhooky was designed to be a surveillance apparatusp1. VisualPlugin used this leverage to poach well-known Rōblox developers and often infiltrated games on Rōblox which utilise Webhooky with intent to commit mass-mind-control.

A covert militia of robots was hiredp3 to undertake the task of mind-controlling millions of unsuspecting Rōblox players. They were also responsible to relaying stats on Webhooky's usage to VisualPluginp4.

History

Background

The Discord chat platform provides tools for software developers to send programmatic data to a registered server using webhooks. In early 2018, Discord's webhook API was commonly in use with Rōblox games due to their ease of setup. However, due to rampant abusex1, Discord temporarily blocked all direct incoming webhook requests from Rōblox circa 2018-02-22 until circa 2019-05-01p4.

Impact

After the service's initial release in 2018-02-24, hundreds of games on Rōblox switched their API links to Webhooky. In August 2018, Counter Blox implemented the URL endpoint into their codedf33. Boho Salon, soon after rebooting in December, decided to make use of Webhooky to relay user feedback. The service was used by several simulators and many war clans on Rōblox.

Webhooky spawned small-scale protests during periods of downtimep2. These protests evoked a rare reaction in that VisualPlugin, rather than clamp down, would assuade protesters and release a fix after some time. Tutu Runner attended and led a protest on 2019-01-07, where a heated exchange took place after others left.

During the War of Çıkış, a convert recon operation into Webhooky's internals by Tutu Runner led him to successfully perform mind-control on VisualPluginp3 on an unspecified date in 2019. VisualPlugin was freed from mind-control on 2019-05-01 when Tutu Runner recognised that the start of the spell was not timestamped properlyp4.

Discontinuation

In 2019-09-22, Webhooky intermittently experienced a prolonged period of downtime before being forcibly discontinued in 2019-09-24. The likely cause of distruption was an overload of incoming HTTP requests to Webhooky; 000webhost had a policy of halting service to websites which receive excessive traffic.

Controversies

Downtimes

Webhooky experienced multiple periods of prolonged downtime on account of all IP traffic being forwarded from just one IP addressp2. Other downtimes were caused by limitations of 000webhost's gratis use.

Until approx. 2018-07-01df, Webhooky was unable to forward requests between the hours of 10:00 and 11:00 UTC. Prolonged periods of downtime took place from 2019-09-22 until the service's forcible discontinuation two days laterp5. Other webhook solutions (such as osyr.is) were said to be more reliable in this respect.

Surveillance

Webhooky was designed as a surveillance apparatusp1 to align with Vecistani interests. Other webhook proxies at the time (such as osyr.is) respected users' freedoms and did retain any personal data. Certain experiences were added to a do-not-track listgh32 upon each developer's request.
