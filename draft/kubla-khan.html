The Kubla Khan

The Kubla Khan Hotel & Resort1 is a hotel map created by the Vecistani Industry of Creative Works that started construction in March 2020. This map is based off architectural plans for the Xanadu2, a 1970s Las Vegas hotel that was never built.

Origin

This map is based off some architectural plans from the 1970s for the Xanadu, a Las Vegas hotel with 'a vaguely Asiatic pleasure-dome theme' that was never built. These plans were digitised under the Dreaming the Skyline collection at UNLV3. The Xanadu was to be built where the Excalibur Hotel & Casino is located today.

Description

The Kubla Khan currently has 1,483 rooms in 18 storeys. Owing to the hotel's unusual shape, each storey has progressively fewer rooms. The room-numbering scheme follows an XXâ€¢YYY scheme, where XX is the floor number and YYY is for the room, both having leading zeroes. Floors or rooms with a four in any digit are skipped, effectively forming a base-9 numbering system. This is as the number four in Chinese numerology is synonymous with death and misfortune. The lighting in-game has been optimised for the 'Future' technology setting.

The final purpose of this game will not be for the player to see every single room, but rather to select one of the room templates that would be inserted in a random location for the player to book for an allotted time. That way, the player will never have millions of blocks in the client at a time - just at most hundreds of thousands. In fact, most hotel games follow the same player-flow model, but to a much smaller and more rudimentary scale. Most of the hotel's components are kept in ServerStorage; whatever 'regions' the client would need are to have rendered during gameplay.
